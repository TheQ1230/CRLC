What We're About

A Brotherhood Built to Serve

This isn't a club — it's a call to action. We're Christian men from Wilmot Township and surrounding areas who refuse to sit back while our neighbours struggle.

  • Connect with believers in churches, ministries, and businesses near you
  • Table-led discussions — every man has a voice, not just a seat
  • Practical commitment to the homeless, widows, orphans, and unemployed
  • Support Canadian missionaries alongside local outreach
  • Become a member with real say in how events are run
  • Start monthly, grow as God provides and more men step up
✝️
How It Works

Come When You Can. Give What You're Able.

You are not obligated to attend every meeting. Come when you're able. The cost is $25 — food, coffee, tax, and tip all included. Every dollar of profit serves people in need.

  • Prayer, singing, an update, a speaker, business highlights, and a missionary or ministry spotlight at every gathering
  • Regular financial statements — hopefully monthly — so everyone sees where funds go
  • All profit goes toward feeding families, missionaries, and helping the unemployed and hurting attend breakfasts

Studies & Resources We Draw From

  • The Heart of a Warrior — a book and teaching course designed specifically for men
  • Alpha — a 10-week course covering the basics of the Christian faith, ideal for new believers
  • Freedom in Christ — a 10-week course for those who want to live a freer, fuller life

About the Founder

Ken Quiggan

Ken became a Christian in August 1991 — and it changed everything. After walking away from a successful but self-centred career, he trusted God with a blank slate.

Over the decades since, Ken has worked with troubled youth at Freedom Village USA, served with Living Rock in Hamilton, set up three group homes for at-risk young people, and mentored young men and women voluntarily for over 25 years.

Ken is an Elder at Wilmot Centre Missionary Church, supported by his wife Anne, a ladies' Bible study leader. Together they serve as altar prayer ministers.

"Bad things happen when good men do nothing. I believe God is calling Christian men in this area to stand up — together."

The Wilmot Christian Men's Group is a natural extension of Ken's lifelong heart for outreach, alongside his work founding the Christian Resource & Learning Centre.
