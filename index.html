For Seekers & Skeptics

Not Sure About Christianity? That's OK.

No pressure. No sales pitch. No judgment. Just honest conversations, free coffee, and real answers to life's biggest questions.

  • A safe, neutral space — not a church — to explore faith on your own terms
  • Ask any question without feeling awkward or judged
  • Meet warm, ordinary people who've wrestled with the same doubts
  • Free Alpha Course — a relaxed 10-week journey through life's big questions
  • Drop in any time — no appointment, no commitment required
  • Find out what local churches actually teach, fairly presented
  • Film nights, guest speakers & community events — fun, not preachy
  • Life skills, freedom-focused programs, and support for real struggles
  • Discover a community that genuinely cares about you as a person
✝️
For Believers & Church Families

Grow Deeper. Connect Wider. Do More Together.

Already following Jesus? The CRLC is a hub to deepen your faith, connect with the wider Christian community, and put your faith into action.

  • Connect with Christians from every denomination in Wilmot Township
  • Access resources, Bible studies, and teaching tools in one place
  • Bring a neighbour or friend who isn't yet a Christian
  • Volunteer to serve, mentor, or lead programs that transform lives
  • Freedom in Christ course — experience deeper healing and wholeness
  • Men's groups, ladies' groups, and young adults gatherings
  • Support outreach events alongside like-minded churches
  • Prayer and healing ministry available
  • Partner with us as a church, business, or individual sponsor

About the Founder

Ken Quiggan

Ken became a Christian in August 1991 — and it changed everything. After a successful but self-centred career in the golf industry, he walked away from it all, trusting God with a blank slate.

Over the following decades, Ken worked with troubled youth at Freedom Village USA, served with Living Rock in Hamilton, set up three group homes for at-risk young people, and has continued mentoring young men and women for over 25 years — voluntarily.

Ken is an Elder at Wilmot Centre Missionary Church and is supported by his wife Anne, a ladies' Bible study leader, and together they are both altar prayer ministers.

"I'm only 75. I have a lot left in me. I want to beat Moses — he started at 80."

This centre is the culmination of a 14-year dream Ken believes God placed on his heart to serve the community of New Hamburg.
